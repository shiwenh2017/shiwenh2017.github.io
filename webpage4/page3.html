2046

2046 takes place in the 1960s still, but this time a few years after the end of In The Mood For Love. The movie centers on Chow (Tony Leung) after his inconsumate relationship with Su. He spent a few years in Malaysia but now has coem back to Hong Kong, in hopes of recovering from his unfulfilled love. He moves into a building into room 2047, one room away from 2046. He meets a number of women over time. Firstly, Lulu (Carina Lau), a dancer who longs for her ex-boyfriend. Then, he encounters Bai Ling (Zhang Ziyi), a carbaret girl who he has a sexual relationship with, but whom develops one-sided feelings for him. He then falls for the building’s owner, Wang Jing-wen (Wong Faye), but his love is unrecquited as she still longs for her Japanese boyfriend. He goes back to Malaysia to look for another Su Li-zhen (Gong Li) he met a few years ago.

2046 is a critically successful film despite not being as world reknowned as In The Mood For Love. 2046 is the last of the films in the trilogy and is an actual sequal to In The Mood For Love. In The Mood For Love is only a loose or indirect sequel to The Days of Being Wild, having a similar setting and similarly named characters. It is interesting to note that 2046 is the only actual sequel that Wong has ever filmed for any one of his films. 2046 is actually more of a sequel in theme to The Days of Bein Wild, because here the main characer is promiscuous and is not binded by any one relationship. The film also no longer focuses on just 2 characters and instead focuses on a number of them. Zhang Ziyi’s performance was a stunning surprise here, with many critics hailing her as a central force in the film. The movie would go onto win many recognitions, including many best film of the year.
