Interests: Music

Music is one of the most important things in my life. I may not play an instrument, but listening to music is one aspect of my life I can hardly imagine living without. When I was just a kid, music was not like this for me, but the older I got, the more important music became. In emotional times as a teenager in high school, music expressed my unbearable and overwhelming emotions. At these times, I listened to Asian music, usually Chinese pop or Japanese pop.



I also listened to some English music, mainly Tupac. In the early, uneasy, and uncomfortable years of college, the years of not fitting in, I listened to instrumentals. Astor Piazzolla and his tango first brought me into or magnified the depths of despair, and later brought me up and out into liberation. Recently, electronic and psychedelic music allowed me to experience in a deeper way, through a more experiential dimension, my psychological and philosophical evolution that I otherwise was only developing cognitively and pushing towards through the efforts of my deliberate attempts at self-development and change.
